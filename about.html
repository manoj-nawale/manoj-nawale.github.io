About Me

I’m Manoj, from Pune, India, now based in Karlsruhe, Germany.

I build data-driven decision systems for real-world operations: optimization models, scenario workflows, and pipelines that teams can trust. I’ve been building things since I was 14, when I made toy cars from scrap parts around home. The parts changed; the instinct didn’t.

I care about clear, explainable work, but I’ve learned that “perfect” is not always the goal. Sometimes the best solution is the one the team can ship, use, and improve together. I still love elegance; I just don’t treat it like religion.

Beyond Work (Yes, I have hobbies that don’t involve Docker logs.)

I read physics for fun, recently quantum field theory, and the idea that particles are field excitations genuinely blew my mind.

I play chess too, with a long-term rating around ~1100. I haven’t crossed that wall yet, and I’m oddly okay with that. It reminds me that progress is not always linear, and patience is part of the game.

I also enjoy open-water swimming, monthly hikes, and mood-regulating playlists that work better than most productivity hacks.

How I Work

I’m a system thinker and builder, but I don’t believe people are “just variables.” Good systems are built by humans, so I try to listen first, then structure.

In teams, I usually contribute by bringing clarity under pressure: translating complexity into actionable decisions, keeping workflows reproducible, and balancing quality with delivery speed.

My default standard is: Make it work. Make it explainable. Then make it elegant. But I adapt that to context, because shipping value with a team beats solo perfection every time.

I’m still improving, especially in knowing when to push harder versus when to move forward pragmatically. I’d rather be honest about that than pretend I’ve already mastered it.

One-line version

I translate complexity into decision-ready systems, from pipelines to models to business action.
